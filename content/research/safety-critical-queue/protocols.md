# Safety-critical population queue — protocol and scoped search plans

Status: protocol planning only. Research proceeds sequentially after the sore-throat pattern is reliable; no reader modules have been drafted.

Shared requirements: define each population precisely; separate respiratory self-care from management of the underlying condition; search current Australian regulator, product-information, pregnancy-medicine, poisons and care-pathway sources; then synthesis databases and primary evidence; extract contraindications/interactions and limits on extrapolation; source every age, dose, duration and escalation threshold; record gaps; require independent clinical review.

1. **Babies and young children:** predefine age bands rather than using “child” broadly; prioritise feeding/hydration, breathing, fever and medicine/poisoning safety. Avoid dose calculation.
2. **Pregnancy and breastfeeding:** treat pregnancy trimester and lactation as distinct; separate maternal illness risk, fetal/infant exposure and formulation-specific medicine evidence.
3. **Older and immunocompromised people:** define older-age study thresholds and immune-suppression causes; do not merge heterogeneous risks; address polypharmacy and atypical presentation as research questions, not assumptions.
4. **Asthma, COPD and chronic respiratory conditions:** separate infection self-care from action plans and exacerbation management; research OTC interactions and escalation pathways without changing prescribed treatment.
5. **Cardiovascular disease and high blood pressure:** define conditions and medicine classes; research decongestant/NSAID interactions and fluid/salt advice without generalising across diagnoses.

For each module the eventual package will contain the complete artefact set in `docs/AGENT_RESEARCH_HANDOFF.md`. Starting all five polished modules from secondary summaries is explicitly prohibited.
