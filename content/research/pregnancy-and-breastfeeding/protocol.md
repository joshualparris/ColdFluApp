# Pregnancy and breastfeeding — research protocol

Status: structured pre-review research only. No canonical module or reader recommendation is created by this package.

## Scope

- Population: people who are pregnant, planning pregnancy or breastfeeding in Australia.
- Core questions: cold versus influenza risk, when to seek care, supportive care, medicine-safety boundaries, breastfeeding, and influenza prevention.
- Exclusions: individual diagnosis, medicine selection or dosing, changes to prescribed medicines, management of pregnancy complications, and infant treatment.

## Outcomes

Keep comfort, symptom relief, illness duration, prevention, maternal harms, fetal or infant harms, breastfeeding outcomes and medical escalation separate. Pregnancy and breastfeeding are distinct contexts; evidence must not be transferred between them without an exact source.

## Eligible sources

Current Australian government, regulator, maternal-medicine and immunisation sources are prioritised for pathways and safety. Systematic reviews are required before benefit or harm magnitudes are proposed. Search snippets and AI output are discovery aids only.

## Review gates

Independent evidence-methods, Australian clinical, and plain-language/accessibility reviews are mandatory. Medicine and escalation wording must remain unpublished until signed approvals are recorded.
