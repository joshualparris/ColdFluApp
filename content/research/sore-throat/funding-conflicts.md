# Sore throat — funding and conflicts

Funding and conflict extraction for the underlying analgesic and medicated-lozenge trials remains outstanding. This is especially material for proprietary lozenge and spray formulations. Organisational authorship is recorded for current guidance, but it does not resolve trial sponsorship risk.

The 2020 Cochrane abstract reports funding sources for only two of nine included trials (government and a university foundation). The ambroxol review's trial-level sponsorship and author conflicts still require full-text extraction; this unresolved risk prevents a reader claim from being added.

The live PubMed record for PMID 34881426 was rechecked on 2026-07-15 and does include an author conflict statement: Anneliese Spinks declared none; Paul Glasziou reported a Therapeutic Guidelines board role and NHMRC antibiotic-resistance grant; Chris Del Mar reported NHMRC and Cochrane funding plus consultancies concerning otitis-media products. These disclosures do not by themselves establish bias or invalidate the review, but an independent reviewer must assess relevance. The earlier statement that PubMed metadata contained no conflict statement for this record was incorrect and is superseded by this paragraph.

Retrieved metadata did not expose usable conflict statements for PMIDs 28931508, 28869700, 3197368, 8491069 or 17535039 in this pass. This means `not reported in retrieved metadata`, not no conflict. Full-text conflict and manufacturer-involvement checks remain required for the AMC/DCBA, ambroxol and individual analgesic/lozenge trials.
