# Sore throat — screening record

Status: initial single-researcher rapid screening, 2026-07-15; not systematic or independently checked.

Included exact records were Healthdirect's sore throat consumer guidance, NICE NG84 and its evidence discussion, and the Australian Government communication on antibiotics for viral respiratory infections. They were selected for Australian course/triage context, transparent guideline synthesis, and Australian antimicrobial-stewardship context respectively.

Search snippets, commercial/product marketing, organisation homepages, laryngitis material, chronic and postoperative sore-throat studies, and AI summaries were excluded. No dual screening occurred. PubMed/MEDLINE, CENTRAL, Epistemonikos, full Cochrane retrieval, and forward/backward citation screening remain incomplete; therefore absence of included evidence must not be read as proof of no evidence.

On 2026-07-15, three preidentified PubMed records were screened using NCBI XML. PMID 32356360 and PMID 24621446 were retained for corticosteroid and ambroxol-lozenge appraisal. PMID 39318911 was excluded from canonical sources because it was a newer Cureus adult corticosteroid review with five studies, very high heterogeneity and no clear advantage over the broader Cochrane update for the current mapping; it remains a discovery lead for forward-search comparison.

The continuation search screened 177 result positions across nine questions (the top 20 per query, except all 17 ibuprofen results). Duplicate records across questions were not formally deduplicated into a PRISMA count, so 177 must not be described as unique citations. Named NICE citations and six specific PMID metadata records were also checked. Five new exact synthesis records are retained in `content/sources/` for research inspection: two corticosteroid syntheses, two formulation-specific lozenge syntheses, and the Cochrane antibiotic review. None supports a new public self-care recommendation without further review.

The oral-analgesia and broad medicated-lozenge canonical claims were removed during screening reconciliation. The underlying material did not justify combining different medicines/formulations, populations and safety profiles into single review-ready claims.
