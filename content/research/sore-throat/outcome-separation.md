# Sore throat outcome separation

This matrix prevents evidence for one outcome from being presented as evidence for another. It describes the current draft and research exclusions; it is not a clinical recommendation.

| Outcome domain | Current reader claim | Evidence boundary | Disposition |
|---|---|---|---|
| Natural history / illness duration | `sore-throat-usual-course` | Describes usual recovery without claiming that supportive care shortens illness. | Retained as an unreviewed draft claim. |
| Comfort | `sore-throat-salt-water-gargle` | Healthdirect recommends it as self-care; direct qualifying trial evidence was not established. Comfort is not cure or duration reduction. | Retained with insufficient certainty and explicit uncertainty. |
| Symptom relief | None | Analgesics and formulation-specific lozenges may affect pain, but directness, harms and formulation mapping are incomplete. | Candidate claims removed pending full extraction and review. |
| Illness-duration treatment effect | None | No retained supportive-care intervention is claimed to shorten the illness. | Do not infer from natural history. |
| Prevention | None | General infection-control statements belong in the household-prevention module and do not prove prevention of an individual sore-throat episode. | Deferred to the prevention module. |
| Harms | Safety caveats only | Medicine adverse effects, choking, interactions and special-population risks need formulation- and population-specific evidence. | No benefit claim may omit the corresponding harms review. |
| Complication prevention | None | Antibiotic evidence depends on cause, baseline risk, era and clinical assessment; it does not support self-diagnosis or self-prescribing. | Kept in research and professional-advice boundaries only. |

The canonical schema already assigns one outcome to each claim. An appointed evidence reviewer must confirm that each future claim remains isolated by population, formulation, comparison and outcome.
