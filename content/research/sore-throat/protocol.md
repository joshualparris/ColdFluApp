# Sore throat supportive care — research protocol

## Identification

- Module ID: `sore-throat`
- Protocol version: 0.2
- Protocol date: 2026-07-15
- Research owner: Codex-assisted draft; accountable human owner unassigned
- Initial jurisdiction: Australia
- Population: adults and children with an acute sore throat in a home/community setting

## Scope

This first slice asks what supportive home care relieves pain or fever, maintains hydration, or changes illness duration, and which features require professional assessment. It does not create a diagnostic rule, antibiotic prescribing guide, or individual dosing schedule.

## Focused questions

1. In people with uncomplicated acute sore throat, what is the usual duration without immediate antibiotics?
2. Compared with placebo or usual care, do oral paracetamol or ibuprofen reduce pain or fever, and what applicability and safety limits matter?
3. Compared with placebo, do medicated or non-medicated lozenges, throat sprays, gargles, warm fluids or other simple measures improve pain or duration?
4. Which symptoms, populations or trajectories should trigger Australian medical or emergency assessment?
5. Which claims about antibiotics can safely be communicated without diagnosing the cause?

## Outcomes

- Primary: throat pain and ability to swallow fluids.
- Secondary: fever, hydration, time to symptom resolution, functional recovery, adverse events and escalation of care.
- Separately labelled: subjective comfort without proven change in illness course.

## Eligibility

- Include current Australian public-health or clinical guidance for local pathways and medicine context.
- Include evidence-based international guidelines where the underlying evidence is transferable.
- Prefer systematic reviews and randomised trials for effectiveness.
- Include all ages, but do not transfer adult evidence to children without explicitly marking indirectness.
- Exclude retailer/manufacturer marketing, unsourced summaries, AI responses as sources, chronic sore throat, post-operative throat pain and treatment requiring a diagnosis unless needed to explain boundaries.
- No arbitrary date cutoff for cornerstone syntheses; search for current guidance and updates through 2026-07-15.

## Search plan

- Australian sources: Healthdirect, Department of Health, Disability and Ageing, TGA, state health services and accessible national guidance.
- Evidence guidance and synthesis: NICE, Cochrane and guideline-linked trials/reviews.
- Use backward and forward searching for any effect estimate promoted into reader content.
- Check publication status, update history, corrections, retractions, applicability and conflicts before publication.

## Certainty approach

Rate each outcome-specific claim separately. Consider study design, risk of bias, consistency, precision, directness, formulation match and clinical importance. Guideline recommendations based mainly on consensus or general safety are not automatically high-certainty effectiveness evidence.

## Deviations and limitations

- This is an initial rapid structured pass, not a completed systematic review.
- Bibliographic database searching and independent dual screening have not been completed.
- Paywalled Australian Therapeutic Guidelines content was not relied on.
- No accountable evidence or clinical reviewer has approved the work.
- The 2026-07-15 continuation used a single researcher and machine-readable PubMed/Europe PMC metadata. Searches were structured but were not independently screened and do not constitute a completed systematic review.
- Broad queries produced substantial irrelevant retrieval. Relevance screening was limited to the first 20 results sorted by PubMed relevance plus named guideline citations and known synthesis records.
- CENTRAL records embedded in Cochrane reviews were inspectable only through abstracts/evidence summaries; independent CENTRAL searching and several proprietary full texts were not accessible.
