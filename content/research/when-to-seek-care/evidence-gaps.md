# When to seek care — evidence gaps

- Independent evidence-methods, Australian emergency/primary-care clinical and plain-language/accessibility reviews are not recorded.
- Emergency, urgent and routine language has not been reconciled across all population packages.
- State and territory service availability, virtual emergency routes and rural access require current verification.
- Pregnancy, postpartum, Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander, disability, aged-care and immunocompromise pathways need dedicated review.
- Exact paediatric age, temperature, feeding, urine and responsiveness thresholds require paediatric clinical sign-off.
- Reader comprehension has not established that urgency levels, non-exhaustive lists and the 000 route are understood.
- No checklist may be used to reassure, diagnose or delay care.

These gaps block canonical integration and publication.
