# When to seek care — research protocol

Status: structured pre-review research only. No canonical triage module or reader recommendation is created by this package.

## Scope

- Population: people in Australia with cold, influenza or other acute respiratory symptoms, with explicit age, pregnancy and medical-risk overlays.
- Core questions: emergency danger signs, urgent deterioration, higher-risk clinical contact, routine care, healthdirect and service navigation.
- Exclusions: diagnosis, risk scoring, reassurance based on absence of a checklist, remote assessment of severity, and replacement of emergency call-taker instructions.

Triage must be symptom-, population- and jurisdiction-specific. A short list can never be presented as exhaustive, and reader concern or rapid change must remain meaningful.

## Outcomes

Keep immediate emergency response, urgent same-day assessment, clinician contact, routine review and self-care monitoring separate.

## Review gates

Independent evidence-methods, Australian emergency/primary-care clinical and plain-language/accessibility reviews are mandatory. Every phone number, age threshold and service route requires current jurisdiction verification immediately before publication.
