# Babies and young children — research protocol

Status: protocol and initial authoritative-source search only. No canonical module or reader recommendation is created by this package.

## Scope

- Population: newborns, infants and children under five, with age bands kept explicit.
- Setting: Australian home observation and decisions to seek professional care for acute respiratory symptoms.
- Core questions: breathing difficulty, feeding, hydration, fever, expected course, smoke exposure, infection spread, medicine boundaries and escalation.
- Exclusions: individual diagnosis, weight-based dosing, diagnosis from symptoms, hospital treatment instructions, vaccination schedules and management of chronic disease.

## Outcomes

Keep comfort, feeding/hydration, illness duration, hospital presentation, respiratory deterioration, adverse effects and transmission separate. Exact ages, temperatures, intake proportions, doses and escalation thresholds require an exact Australian source plus independent clinical review.

## Eligible sources

1. Current Australian government, paediatric hospital, regulator, poisons and clinical-guideline sources for safety and pathways.
2. Current systematic reviews or evidence-based guidelines for intervention benefits and harms.
3. Trials only when synthesis is absent or an update may change conclusions.

Commercial product pages, search snippets, unsourced parenting content and AI answers are discovery aids only.

## Initial search plan

- Official domains: Healthdirect, Pregnancy Birth and Baby, Royal Children's Hospital Melbourne, Sydney Children's Hospitals Network, NHMRC, Australian Government Department of Health, TGA and state health services.
- Evidence synthesis: Cochrane Library and PubMed for bronchiolitis supportive care, saline, suction, feeding/hydration, smoke exposure and medicine harms.
- Safety overlays: fever in young infants, breathing difficulty, poor feeding/dehydration, prematurity, chronic cardiopulmonary/neurological disease and immunocompromise.

## Review gates

Independent evidence review, Australian paediatric/clinical review, and plain-language/accessibility review are mandatory. This module must not inherit sore-throat approval or advance from research based on AI review.
