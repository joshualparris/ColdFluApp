# Babies and young children — evidence and safety gaps

- Age bands and definitions are not yet normalised across newborns, young infants, older infants and children under five.
- No independent duplicate screening or extraction has occurred.
- Medicine safety now has initial TGA antihistamine/paracetamol and national Poisons Information Centre records; other active ingredients, product restrictions and accidental double-dose scenarios remain unresearched.
- Fever, apnoea, breathing effort, feeding and dehydration thresholds require paediatric clinical review.
- Initial Cochrane records for OTC cough medicines, honey and nasal saline are extracted. Infant saline/suction directness, humidification, feeding support and newer updates remain to be systematically searched.
- Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander applicability and service pathways require appropriate accountable input.
- Correction/retraction, funding and conflict checks are not complete.
- Four candidate safety mappings exist, but no canonical claim, evidence grade or publication date has been created.
