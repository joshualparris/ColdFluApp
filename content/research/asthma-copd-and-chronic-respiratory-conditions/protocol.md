# Asthma, COPD and chronic respiratory conditions — research protocol

Status: structured pre-review research only. No canonical module or reader recommendation is created by this package.

## Scope

- Populations: people in Australia with asthma, COPD or another diagnosed chronic respiratory condition.
- Core questions: respiratory infections as flare-up triggers, change from personal baseline, existing action plans, urgent escalation, prevention and medicine-safety boundaries.
- Exclusions: diagnosing asthma or COPD, creating a personal action plan, changing prescribed inhalers, initiating antibiotics or corticosteroids, oxygen instructions, and reproducing emergency medicine doses.

Asthma and COPD are distinct conditions. Other respiratory diagnoses must not inherit their action-plan or medicine instructions without a direct source and appropriate specialist review.

## Outcomes

Keep infection prevention, asthma or COPD exacerbation, symptom comfort, illness duration, hospitalisation, medicine harms and emergency escalation separate.

## Review gates

Independent evidence-methods, Australian respiratory clinical and plain-language/accessibility reviews are mandatory. Any emergency wording must be reconciled with current national first-aid and condition-specific guidance.
