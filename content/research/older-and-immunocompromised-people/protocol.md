# Older and immunocompromised people — research protocol

Status: structured pre-review research only. No canonical module or reader recommendation is created by this package.

## Scope

- Populations: adults aged 65 years and older, people with mild through severe immunocompromise, carers and close contacts in Australia.
- Core questions: risk of severe influenza, atypical or worsening illness, when to contact a clinician, prevention, household protection, medicine interactions and treatment-access planning.
- Exclusions: diagnosing influenza from symptoms, defining a person's degree of immunocompromise, changing immunosuppressive therapy, selecting vaccine products, antiviral prescribing or dosing, and residential-facility outbreak management.

Age alone and immunocompromise are separate risk dimensions. Neither population is homogeneous, and advice must account for frailty, comorbidities, medicines, transplant or treatment timing, and the person's existing clinical plan.

## Outcomes

Keep symptom comfort, illness duration, prevention, hospitalisation, mortality, medicine harms, transmission and escalation separate.

## Review gates

Independent evidence-methods, Australian clinical and plain-language/accessibility reviews are mandatory. Specialist review is required for immunocompromise, transplant, cancer therapy and medicine-interaction wording.
