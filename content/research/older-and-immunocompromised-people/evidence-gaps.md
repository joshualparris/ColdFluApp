# Older and immunocompromised people — evidence gaps

- Independent evidence-methods, Australian clinical and plain-language/accessibility reviews are not recorded.
- Antiviral benefit, harm, timing and interaction evidence has not been systematically reviewed.
- The module does not yet distinguish all causes, severities and treatment phases of immunocompromise.
- Evidence on atypical presentation, frailty, cognitive change and treatment failure is incomplete.
- OTC medicine interactions, anticholinergic burden, renal or hepatic impairment and polypharmacy require exact current sources and clinical review.
- Residential aged-care outbreak pathways and prolonged infectiousness are outside this checkpoint.
- Funding, conflicts, corrections and retractions remain to be checked for future study-level sources.
- Comfort, symptom relief, illness duration, prevention, transmission, hospitalisation and mortality must remain separate.

These gaps block canonical integration and publication.
