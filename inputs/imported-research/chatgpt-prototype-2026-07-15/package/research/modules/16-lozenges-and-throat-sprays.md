# 16. Lozenges and throat sprays

**Section:** B. Interventions  
**Reviewed:** 2026-07-15  

> **Educational information only.** This page does not diagnose illness or replace advice from a doctor, pharmacist, nurse or emergency service.

## Summary

Lozenges and sprays can provide short-term sore-throat relief but do not treat the underlying virus. Ingredient choice matters because anaesthetics, anti-inflammatories, antiseptics and zinc have different benefits and risks.

## Evidence snapshot

Local symptom relief is plausible and supported for some medicated products, but effects are temporary and formulation-specific.

## What actually helps

### Simple soothing lozenges — Moderate

**Finding:** Increase saliva and reduce dryness and irritation.

**Mechanism:** Saliva lubricates irritated tissue.

**Practical use:** Use as directed; sugar-free options reduce dental exposure.

**Cautions:** Choking risk in young children.

### Local anaesthetic lozenges/sprays — Moderate

**Finding:** Can numb pain for a limited period.

**Mechanism:** Blocks local nerve conduction.

**Practical use:** Use label frequency and avoid eating immediately if swallowing feels numb.

**Cautions:** Overuse can irritate tissue or impair safe swallowing.

### Anti-inflammatory lozenges — Moderate

**Finding:** Some ingredients such as flurbiprofen can reduce throat pain.

**Mechanism:** Local NSAID effect.

**Practical use:** Ask a pharmacist if NSAIDs are unsafe for you.

**Cautions:** NSAID cautions still apply.

### Antiseptic products — Weak-or-mixed

**Finding:** May reduce microbes in the mouth but have not been shown to cure a viral sore throat.

**Mechanism:** Local antiseptic action.

**Practical use:** Use for comfort, not as an antibiotic substitute.

**Cautions:** Allergy and irritation possible.

### Zinc lozenges — Weak-or-mixed

**Finding:** May shorten a cold in some trials but causes taste disturbance and nausea; optimal formulation is uncertain.

**Mechanism:** Possible local antiviral effect.

**Practical use:** See the natural-remedies module.

**Cautions:** Do not use intranasal zinc.

## Practical steps

- Match the product to the symptom: soothing, numbing or anti-inflammatory.
- Check age restrictions and active ingredients.
- Use pain relief and fluids as the foundation rather than stacking many throat products.

## Who should avoid it or take extra care

- Do not give hard lozenges to children who cannot manage them safely.
- Persistent severe throat pain despite numbing needs assessment.

## Common myths

- **Myth:** Antiseptic lozenges sterilise the throat.  
  **Reality:** The throat cannot be safely sterilised, and most sore throats are viral.
- **Myth:** If numbness is stronger, the product is treating the cause.  
  **Reality:** Numbness is symptom relief only.

## Red flags

- Difficulty swallowing saliva or breathing
- New swelling of lips/tongue or wheeze
- Severe one-sided pain or neck swelling

## Unsettled science / evidence gaps

- Head-to-head evidence between common Australian lozenge ingredients is limited.
