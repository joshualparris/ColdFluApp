# Research roadmap: expanding beyond the first 28 modules

The initial release covers the user-requested 28 modules. It is deliberately not presented as "every remedy known to humanity." The next phase should broaden coverage while keeping each question narrow enough to evaluate rigorously.

## Priority 1 — high safety impact

1. **COVID-19 home management and Australian antiviral eligibility**
   - current testing;
   - risk groups;
   - timing windows;
   - medicine interactions;
   - current isolation/precaution advice.

2. **RSV, bronchiolitis and infant respiratory distress**
   - age-specific breathing signs;
   - feeding/hydration;
   - suction evidence;
   - prevention and immunisation products in Australia.

3. **Pneumonia and serious bacterial-complication recognition**
   - diagnostic limitations of symptoms;
   - "double worsening";
   - chest pain, hypoxia and sepsis signs;
   - when imaging is useful.

4. **Medication-interaction engine**
   - paracetamol duplication;
   - decongestants with cardiovascular/psychiatric medicines;
   - dextromethorphan with serotonergic medicines;
   - sedatives and antihistamines;
   - NSAIDs with anticoagulants, kidneys, ulcers and heart failure.

5. **Kidney disease, liver disease and diabetes**
   - medicine selection;
   - sick-day plans;
   - dehydration;
   - glucose monitoring;
   - when to contact the treating team.

## Priority 2 — commonly used interventions needing deeper formulation-specific review

6. Zinc lozenges: salt, elemental dose, timing, duration and adverse taste/nausea.
7. Vitamin C: routine prevention versus treatment after onset; general population versus high physical stress.
8. Vitamin D: deficiency correction versus acute treatment.
9. Honey: adult versus child outcomes, dose and comparison products.
10. Echinacea: species, plant part, extraction method and product standardisation.
11. Elderberry: trial quality, publication bias and safety.
12. Pelargonium sidoides, ivy leaf, andrographis and other marketed herbal products.
13. Ginger, garlic, turmeric and capsaicin: clinical outcomes versus laboratory mechanisms.
14. Probiotics and prebiotics for prevention, duration and adverse effects.
15. Nasal carrageenan, xylitol, nitric-oxide products and other antiviral nasal sprays.
16. Gargles: salt, povidone-iodine and other antiseptics — efficacy and safety.
17. Menthol, camphor and vapour rubs: subjective airflow, sleep and poisoning risk.
18. Humidifier type, cleaning protocol and humidity measurement.
19. Sauna, hot baths and whole-body heating.
20. Cold exposure, contrast showers and deliberate temperature stress.

## Priority 3 — practical recovery and function

21. Expected symptom timeline by pathogen and age.
22. Return to work, school, childcare and exercise.
23. Post-viral cough and when it becomes chronic cough.
24. Post-viral fatigue and pacing.
25. Sleep disruption: nocturnal cough, nasal congestion and caregiver sleep.
26. Voice rest, laryngitis and professional voice use.
27. Air travel, altitude and ear/sinus pressure during illness.
28. Oral health, dry mouth and mouth breathing.
29. Appetite loss, nausea and diarrhoea during respiratory infection.
30. Caregiver burden, family routines and reducing household conflict while everyone is sick.

## Priority 4 — diagnostics and home monitoring

31. Rapid antigen tests, PCR and timing/false negatives.
32. Thermometer accuracy by age and measurement site.
33. Pulse oximeters: accuracy, skin pigmentation, nail products and thresholds.
34. Respiratory-rate measurement in children.
35. Smartwatch heart rate and illness.
36. Home blood-pressure monitoring while using decongestants.
37. Symptom scores and daily trajectory tracking.
38. When throat swabs, chest X-rays or sinus imaging are appropriate.

## Priority 5 — prevention

39. Indoor air cleaners and HEPA filtration.
40. Ventilation rate and carbon-dioxide monitoring as a proxy.
41. Mask type, fit and household source control.
42. Hand hygiene versus surface cleaning by respiratory virus.
43. School and childcare outbreak practice.
44. Influenza vaccine effectiveness by age and season.
45. COVID-19 vaccination and co-administration.
46. Smoking/vaping cessation and airway infection risk.
47. Sleep duration and infection susceptibility.
48. Exercise dose and upper-respiratory infection risk.
49. Stress, loneliness and immune outcomes without psychologising illness.
50. Nutrition deficiency screening versus universal supplementation.

## Research queue format

Create one issue per question with:

- module/parent module;
- PICO/SPIDER/PECO question;
- safety priority;
- expected source hierarchy;
- assigned researcher;
- search date;
- status: scoped / searching / extracting / audited / merged;
- unresolved contradictions;
- next review date.

## Principle

Coverage should grow by adding well-defined modules and submodules, not by placing every conceivable claim on one page. A larger knowledge base is only more useful when provenance, uncertainty and safety remain visible.
