# 28. Cold vs influenza vs COVID-19 vs strep throat vs allergies

**Section:** E. Triage  
**Reviewed:** 2026-07-15  

> **Educational information only.** This page does not diagnose illness or replace advice from a doctor, pharmacist, nurse or emergency service.

## Summary

Symptoms overlap heavily, so a table can guide suspicion but cannot make a diagnosis. Onset pattern, fever, itch, exposure, testing and examination provide the most useful distinctions.

## Evidence snapshot

Influenza often starts abruptly with prominent systemic symptoms; allergies commonly cause itch and repeated sneezing without fever; strep classically causes sore throat and fever without cough. COVID-19 can resemble either a cold or flu and may require testing.

## What actually helps

### Use pattern recognition cautiously — Moderate

**Finding:** Combinations of symptoms shift probability but are not definitive.

**Mechanism:** Different diseases have typical but overlapping inflammatory patterns.

**Practical use:** Compare onset, fever, cough, nasal itch, aches and exposures.

**Cautions:** Young children, older adults and immunocompromised people may present atypically.

### Targeted testing — Strong

**Finding:** Can identify COVID-19, influenza or group A streptococcus when the result would change treatment or precautions.

**Mechanism:** Detects pathogen antigen, nucleic acid or immune response.

**Practical use:** Follow current Australian testing advice and clinician guidance.

**Cautions:** A negative test can be falsely reassuring if timing or sampling is poor.

### Clinical examination — Strong

**Finding:** Assesses tonsils, lymph nodes, breathing, hydration, ears and complications.

**Mechanism:** Adds objective findings and differential diagnosis.

**Practical use:** Seek review for severe sore throat, breathing difficulty, persistent fever or high-risk status.

**Cautions:** Online symptom comparison cannot detect every complication.

## Practical steps

- Treat the comparison table as a probability guide, not a verdict.
- Test when a result changes isolation, antivirals, antibiotics or contact with vulnerable people.
- Reassess if the illness changes course or lasts longer than expected.
- Antibiotics are for confirmed or strongly suspected bacterial disease, not routine colds, influenza or COVID-19.

## Who should avoid it or take extra care

- Do not diagnose strep only from white spots; viral tonsillitis can look similar.
- Do not rule out COVID-19 because symptoms are mild or include a runny nose.
- Do not assume loss of smell is unique to COVID-19; severe congestion and other causes can contribute.

## Common myths

- **Myth:** Body aches always mean influenza.  
  **Reality:** They are more typical and often stronger in influenza, but COVID-19 and other infections can also cause them.
- **Myth:** No cough means strep throat.  
  **Reality:** Absence of cough is only one feature; testing or clinical scoring is needed when strep is suspected.

## Red flags

- Drooling, inability to swallow saliva, muffled voice, neck swelling or breathing difficulty
- Severe one-sided throat pain, trismus or rapidly worsening swelling
- Breathlessness, chest pain, confusion, dehydration or blue/grey colour
- High-risk person with possible influenza or COVID-19 who may qualify for early treatment

## Unsettled science / evidence gaps

- Symptom profiles change with variants, vaccination, prior immunity, age and co-infection.

## Symptom comparison

| feature | Common cold | Influenza | COVID-19 | Strep throat | Allergies |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Typical onset | Gradual | Often abrupt | Variable | Often abrupt throat pain | After exposure; recurrent/seasonal |
| Fever | Uncommon or low-grade | Common, often prominent | Possible | Common | No |
| Runny/blocked nose | Very common | Possible | Common in many cases | Usually absent | Very common; often watery/itchy |
| Cough | Common | Common, often dry | Common | Usually absent | Possible from postnasal drip |
| Sore throat | Common | Possible | Common | Prominent | Mild irritation possible |
| Aches/fatigue | Mild | Often marked | Variable, sometimes marked | Possible but less dominant | Usually absent except poor sleep |
| Itchy eyes/nose | Unusual | Unusual | Unusual | No | Characteristic |
| Useful next step | Home care unless red flags | Early antiviral assessment if high-risk/severe | Test/precautions and early treatment assessment if eligible | Clinical assessment and test when indicated | Trigger control and allergy treatment |
