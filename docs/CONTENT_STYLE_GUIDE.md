# Content style guide (draft)

Use Australian English and plain, direct sentences. Explain medical terms on first use. Describe symptoms without claiming a diagnosis and never imply that symptom comparison can establish a cause.

Keep comfort, symptom relief, illness duration, prevention, complications and harms distinct. Do not call comfort a cure. Avoid marketing language, superlatives, brand promotion and the implication that “natural” means safe. State uncertainty visibly and match every claim to the studied population, active ingredient, formulation, timing and outcome. Prefer absolute effects with denominators and timeframes where reasonably available; do not turn statistical significance into clinical importance.

Safety wording should name the action and timeframe without alarmism: “call triple zero (000) now”, “seek urgent assessment today”, or “ask a pharmacist before use”. Exact ages, doses, durations and escalation thresholds require an exact source and clinical review. Global evidence and Australian rules must be labelled separately.

Drafts say what is unresolved. Never use `reviewed`, `approved`, `safe`, `works`, or `published` unless the record supports that exact status and scope. Reviewer expiry wording follows the approved policy when one exists; the current proposal in `CONTROLLED_VOCABULARIES.md` is not yet approved.
