# Scope and module map

## Initial release scope

The initial catalogue contains 28 modules. This is a starting taxonomy, not a fixed information architecture.

### Symptoms

1. Cough: dry and productive
2. Sore throat
3. Runny or blocked nose
4. Fever
5. Fatigue and body aches
6. Sinus pressure and headache
7. Loss of taste or smell
8. Ear pressure or blockage

### Interventions

9. Hydration
10. Humidity and steam
11. Rest, sleep, and positioning
12. Nutrition during illness
13. OTC medicines by active ingredient
14. Complementary and traditional remedies
15. Gargles, saline sprays, and nasal irrigation
16. Lozenges and throat sprays
17. Warm compresses
18. Prescription antivirals and timing

### Populations and conditions

19. Babies and young children
20. Pregnancy and breastfeeding
21. Older and immunocompromised people
22. Asthma, COPD, and chronic respiratory conditions
23. Cardiovascular disease and high blood pressure

### Prevention and household

24. Hand hygiene, surfaces, ventilation, and household precautions
25. Sleep, activity, stress, and general health
26. Influenza vaccination

### Decisions and triage

27. Self-care, pharmacist, primary care, urgent care, or emergency care
28. Cold, influenza, COVID-19, strep throat, and allergy comparison

## Required expansion before claiming broad usefulness

- Separate pregnancy from breastfeeding where recommendations differ.
- Add renal disease, liver disease, diabetes, anticoagulant use, and polypharmacy overlays.
- Treat COVID-19 as a maintained topic rather than only a comparison row.
- Include medicine poisoning and accidental double-dose guidance by jurisdiction.
- Add health-literacy, disability, carer, and low-resource perspectives.
- Define jurisdiction packs beyond Australia only when reviewed locally.

## Content boundaries

Each symptom module may explain supportive home care, common trajectories, relevant tests, and escalation signals. It must not assert a diagnosis from symptoms alone.

Each intervention module must distinguish:

- prevention from treatment;
- adults from children and special populations;
- statistical findings from clinically meaningful benefit;
- studied formulation, route, dose, and timing from unlike retail products;
- short-term use from repeated or long-term exposure.

Triage content is safety-critical and must be independently reviewed, highly visible, and localised. Emergency warnings must not depend on JavaScript, search, or completion of a questionnaire.

## Publication units

A “module” is a reader-facing topic. Within it, every evidence rating applies to an individual claim. Sources are reusable records referenced by claims. Jurisdiction and population overlays can amend—not silently overwrite—core content.
