# Medical glossary (draft)

| Term | Plain-language meaning |
|---|---|
| Acute | Starts recently and usually lasts a limited time; it does not mean severe. |
| Adverse effect | An unwanted effect associated with an intervention or medicine. |
| Analgesic | A medicine used to reduce pain. |
| Antimicrobial resistance | When microbes change so medicines used against them become less effective. |
| Contraindication | A circumstance in which an intervention should not be used because risk may be unacceptable. |
| Evidence certainty | Confidence in the evidence for one specific effect estimate or conclusion. |
| Formulation | The particular physical and ingredient form, such as tablet, syrup, lozenge or spray. |
| Immunocompromised | Having reduced immune defence because of a condition or treatment; degree and implications vary. |
| Indirect evidence | Evidence that differs from the question in population, intervention, comparison, outcome or setting. |
| Placebo | An inactive or comparison treatment designed to resemble the intervention being studied. |
| Self-limiting | Usually improves without treatment directed at the cause; this does not remove the need to watch for warning signs. |
| Symptom relief | A reduction in how troublesome a symptom feels; it may not shorten the illness. |
| Systematic review | A review using predefined methods to find, assess and combine relevant studies. |

Definitions support plain-language consistency and are not diagnostic advice. Clinical and editorial reviewers must approve the final glossary.
