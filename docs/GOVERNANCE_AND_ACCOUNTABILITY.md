## Proposed conflict and attribution policy

Reviewers disclose relevant employment, consultancy, research funding, honoraria, intellectual-property interests, advocacy roles and relationships with manufacturers or publishers. The project owner records whether a conflict is manageable, requires an independent second review, or disqualifies the reviewer for the affected scope. Public names and qualifications are shown only with consent; internal accountability records remain required.

## Proposed funding and commercial policy

No paid ranking, affiliate link, sponsored claim, undisclosed native advertising or manufacturer control of evidence conclusions is permitted. Funding and in-kind support must be disclosed with its scope and conditions. Advertising, if ever considered, requires a new decision record, separation from medical content and legal/clinical review. Supplementary media must not be selected in exchange for value.

## Proposed corrections and emergency-unpublishing policy

- Critical: plausible immediate risk of serious harm. Unpublish or disable affected content immediately when authorised; notify the clinical reviewer and owner without delay.
- High: material safety, contraindication, triage or dosing error without immediate widespread danger. Remove or prominently correct as soon as practicable and target an accountable decision within one business day.
- Moderate: materially misleading effectiveness, certainty, applicability or source statement. Target assessment within five business days.
- Low: clarity, formatting or non-material metadata problem. Correct through normal maintenance.

Any contributor may raise a correction. Only the named owner or documented delegate may close a material incident. A clinical reviewer must assess clinical impact. Corrections record the affected version, report date, assessment, action, approver and public notice where applicable. Continued uncertainty favours temporary unpublishing when plausible harm is material.

## Approval rule

Draft policy text is not approval. An approver must record their name, role, decision, date, scope, conflicts and the exact commit/version approved. Until then, publication and public launch remain blocked.
