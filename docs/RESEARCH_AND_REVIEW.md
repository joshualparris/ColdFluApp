# Research and review workflow

## Roles

- **Researcher:** frames questions, searches, screens, extracts, and drafts.
- **Evidence reviewer:** checks search coverage, source applicability, extraction, certainty, and unsupported inference.
- **Clinical reviewer:** checks medical accuracy, dosing, contraindications, interactions, triage, and population safety.
- **Editor:** checks plain language, consistency, accessibility, and avoidance of diagnostic certainty.
- **Publisher:** confirms all gates and releases the version.

One person may hold multiple roles, but the clinical approval of safety-critical content must be independent from AI generation. Reviewer identity, qualifications relevant to the review, conflicts, and review date should be recorded internally; public attribution requires consent.

## Research protocol per module

1. **Frame questions.** Use PICO for intervention questions and an appropriate diagnostic framework for symptom differentiation. Define population, setting, formulation, timing, comparison, and outcomes.
2. **Predefine criteria.** Record inclusion/exclusion rules, date range, languages, jurisdictions, and eligible designs before searching.
3. **Search authoritative guidance.** Start with current national guidelines and regulators for safety, scheduling, and local pathways.
4. **Search evidence syntheses.** Seek systematic reviews and meta-analyses, then pivotal or newer trials that change conclusions.
5. **Snowball deliberately.** Check references and forward citations for cornerstone sources.
6. **Log the search.** Save databases/sites, exact queries, dates, filters, and result counts so the search is reproducible.
7. **Screen and extract.** Capture population, intervention, comparison, outcomes, study design, effect, adverse events, limitations, funding, and applicability.
8. **Assess certainty.** Consider risk of bias, consistency, precision, directness, publication bias, and whether benefit is clinically meaningful.
9. **Draft structured claims.** Populate the schema directly. Never convert a paper abstract into a stronger claim than the paper supports.
10. **Run independent reviews.** Evidence, clinical, editorial, accessibility, and link checks occur before publication.

## Source priority

Source choice depends on the claim:

1. Current local government, regulator, poison service, or professional guideline for urgent pathways, medicine rules, vaccination, and public-health advice.
2. High-quality systematic reviews and evidence-based guidelines for effectiveness and harms.
3. Peer-reviewed trials or observational evidence where synthesis is absent or outdated.
4. Official product information for formulation-specific contraindications and approved use.
5. Reputable tertiary summaries only for orientation or when they accurately relay primary material.

Search-engine summaries, retailer pages, unsourced wellness content, and AI answers are discovery aids, not evidence records.

## Required evidence artefacts

Each module folder should eventually contain or link to:

- question and protocol;
- search log;
- screening decisions;
- extraction table;
- risk-of-bias or applicability notes;
- claim-to-source map;
- reviewer findings and resolutions;
- version and change history.

Respect copyright: store bibliographic metadata, lawful notes, and links unless the project has rights to retain full text.

## AI use policy

AI may help generate queries, deduplicate citations, extract candidate data, compare drafts, and identify missing considerations. Every AI-produced citation and factual claim must be checked against the source. AI output is never itself a source. Record the model/tool and date for material AI-assisted work so errors can be audited.

## Publication gates

`draft -> evidence_review -> clinical_review -> editorial_review -> published`

A failed gate returns the module to `draft` with actionable findings. No draft can skip a gate because another module with similar content passed.

## Maintenance and corrections

- Give time-sensitive modules shorter review intervals.
- Monitor regulators, retractions/corrections, major guideline changes, and safety alerts.
- Provide a visible correction channel.
- Triage reported safety issues immediately; unpublish content when continued availability could plausibly harm readers.
- Publish material corrections with date, scope, and impact rather than silently replacing them.
