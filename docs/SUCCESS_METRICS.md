# Privacy-preserving success metrics

The project will not measure success by collecting symptoms, diagnoses, treatment choices or individual health histories.

## Pre-launch measures

- Publication-gate escape count: target zero unreviewed modules exposed publicly.
- Source traceability: 100% of material claims map to exact reusable source records.
- Review completeness: 100% of published modules have current evidence, clinical and editorial approvals.
- Safety-source coverage: 100% of urgent and emergency statements have current jurisdiction-specific authoritative sources.
- Automated quality: required CI and production checks pass on every public release.
- Accessibility: no unresolved critical WCAG 2.2 A/AA findings and documented manual assistive-technology testing.

## Human-study measures

Reader-comprehension testing should measure, in aggregate, whether participants can distinguish comfort from treatment, locate urgent-care advice, identify jurisdiction and uncertainty, and find supporting sources. Predefine thresholds with the accountable clinical and editorial reviewers. Retain only the minimum de-identified aggregate results necessary for product decisions.

## Operational measures

- Time from a credible critical safety report to temporary unpublishing.
- Time from correction acceptance to reviewed resolution.
- Percentage of modules reviewed before expiry.
- Production availability and failed operational-assurance checks.
- Backup restore success and elapsed recovery time.

## Prohibited or deferred measurement

Do not add session replay, advertising identifiers, cross-site tracking, symptom analytics, user profiles or inferred health segments. Basic aggregate traffic measurement requires a documented privacy decision, data-flow review, retention period and opt-out assessment before implementation.
