# External launch prerequisites

This register contains work that cannot be completed or honestly approved by a coding or research assistant. It is deliberately separate from `TODO.md`, which tracks executable repository work. Removing these items from the working backlog does not waive them: the publication validator and governance rules continue to block unreviewed medical content.

## Accountable appointments

The project owner must obtain and record accepted appointments for:

- a named legal/project owner with launch and correction authority;
- an Australian clinical reviewer with relevant current qualifications;
- an evidence-methods reviewer with relevant synthesis experience; and
- a plain-language/accessibility editor with relevant health-literacy and accessibility experience.

Each appointment requires the person's identity, relevant qualifications, scope, conflicts, attribution consent, acceptance date, appointing authority, review date and evidence of acceptance. Vacancies remain recorded in `docs/GOVERNANCE_AND_ACCOUNTABILITY.md`.

## Independent module approvals

Every canonical module, beginning with sore throat, requires review performed and signed by the appointed people:

1. evidence review of search coverage, eligibility, extraction, claim-source mapping, certainty, funding, conflicts, corrections and retractions;
2. Australian clinical review of accuracy, doses, ages, contraindications, interactions, duration limits, vulnerable populations and escalation thresholds;
3. plain-language and accessibility review of comprehension, ambiguity, structure, alternative text and safety communication; and
4. publisher confirmation that findings were resolved against an identified commit.

AI assistance, automated checks, draft review packets and repository maintainers cannot satisfy these approvals.

## Policy approvals

The accountable roles identified in `docs/GOVERNANCE_AND_ACCOUNTABILITY.md` must approve the evidence vocabulary, review-expiry intervals, reviewer conflicts and attribution, funding/commercial policy, corrections and emergency unpublishing, and reviewer-signature procedure. Draft text is not approval.

## Human and environment-dependent assurance

Before public launch, accountable people must complete and retain evidence for:

- reader-comprehension testing with representative participants;
- NVDA and VoiceOver testing on supported browser/operating-system combinations;
- independent accessibility, privacy and security assessments;
- Australian legal advice covering health information, consumer representations, accessibility, privacy, attribution and corrections;
- assignment and acceptance of service ownership, incident authority and correction escalation contacts; and
- any Vercel account action that requires an account owner, including credential rotation and plan-dependent deployment protection.

## Completion rule

A prerequisite is complete only when the repository links to a dated record identifying scope, exact commit, reviewer or owner, qualifications or authority, conflicts, findings, resolutions, limitations and signature or other evidence of acceptance. Until all applicable records exist, public medical-content publication and launch remain blocked.
